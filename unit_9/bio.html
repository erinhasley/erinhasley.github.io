It seems as if I have always had a passion for making things. As a child, I developed the habit of reading about craft techniques and then experimenting until I could produce satisfactory results. Eventually, I became interested in printmaking and worked with woodblock, silkscreen and white line techniques. For several years, papermaking became my primary artistic interest. More recently, I learned about acrylics and creative uses of paper at the Avenue Road School of Art and was introduced to encaustic techniques at the Art Barn in Toronto. After each of these learning opportunities, I was inspired to take up my old habits of reading and experimenting.


Working in encaustic appeals to me because it allows me to express a connection with the natural environment. It can be a very unpredictable medium, but I find the element of surprise intriguing. The finished product contains a germ of the original idea, but is a unique work.
